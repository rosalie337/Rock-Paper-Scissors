Rock, Paper, Scissors (aka "Ro-Sham-Bo"; janken; "Bato, Bato, Pick"; and "Scissors, Paper, Stone") is a simple hand game with many names and variations. It is played around the world and is commonly used as a way of coming to decisions. In some cases is even played for sport. The rules require that competing players use one hand to form one of three shapes at an agreed-upon time.

What to know:

  • rock beats scissors
  • scissors beats paper
  • paper beats rock

The person that plays the strongest “object” is the winner of the game. It's that easy!
